Opening Duels

At some point in every round of every match in Valorant, a gunfight will occur between opposing players. The first decisive gunfight (one in which a player dies) in each round is known as the opening duel. Suppose the first combat action of a round is Player A killing Player B. Then, Player A earns a First Blood (FB) and Player B is given a First Death (FD). Exactly one FB and one FD is given in each round.

Naturally, the opening duel of a Valorant round is considered an extremely important event. Which its significance varies depending on the result (whether the attacker or defender wins the duel and/or whether the killer is traded), a team will always hope to get that first kill and create a 5v4 situation. But let's dive deeper into the data and see just how important it is.

First, we'll go through some summary statistics. The term success rate will be used to refer to the proportion of opening duels won by a given team.
